Abstract

Base registries are todo. Until today, maintaining a base registry comes with extra maintenance costs to create the dataset and keep it up to date. We study the possibility to entangle the maintenance of base registries at the core of existing administrative processes, to reduce the cost of maintaining a new data source. In this paper, we demo a way to manage local decisions as linked data which creates a new base registry for mandates. We found that no extra effort was needed in the process by local administrations. We show that an end-to-end approach for Local Decisions as Linked Data is feasible. Furthermore, we obtained political leverage to implement the ideas behind this PoC and roll it out for the region of Flanders in Belgium.

Introduction

Councils are empowered by law, to make decisions on many matters of importance to their local communities. Decisions are made in formally constituted council meetings. Local government data, digitizing these decisions, is locked in data silo's and therefore manually registered in the authorative data source of the regional government. Imagine a smart city where public decision-making is easy for all to follow using any digital channel. s.a. "the effects of local tax policy" or an insight in "the public procurement".

This pilot is a stepstone in the transition of the Flemish Agency for Domestic Governance towards an administration with a greater focus on policy development in terms of better administrative processes.

Related work

Within European policies today, there is on-going work towards base registries, trusted datasets, authoritative sources and appointing responsible parties to collect certain information. A base registry (BR) is a trusted authentic source of information under the control of an appointed public administration or organisation appointed by government [1] trusted means that the local government is managing the local council decisions conformant to best practices in all EIF-domains [2], more specific at the level of semantic and organizational interoperability and conformant to legal requirements (Flemish Decree *?). On the semantic level we’ve defined a vocabulary for council decisions and the organizational level and we’ve organized working groups with the local and regional governments, where they have agreed on decentralized processes which provide data to the base registry, using the principles of linked data. authentic means that this is considered to be THE source of information which represents the correct status and which kept constantly up-to-date and is of highest possible quality. This is archieved by avoiding public servants to copy the information into a separated form or application. When the registered data is part of the core processes of the local administrations and used in their own information systems, we expect this will improve the quality. under control means that all measures have been taken that only parties that have a necessity & finality & authorisation can access the information in proportionality with their needs. (nope, the POC we’ve focused on data not under privacy regulations) appointed means: that the governing administration, the Flemish Agency for Domestic Governance, has a legal basis to collect and maintain the respective information.

Method and implementation

We interviewed X service providers for their stance on Linked Data, interviewed Y local governments on how they register and publish local council decisions. We also organized 3 workshops which formed the input for a software engineer to create a proof of concept. The domain concepts were formalized into two Linked Data vocabularies: one for the metadata and one for describing public mandates. The proof of consists of 5 components: an editor for local decisions, an HTML-page publishing service responsible for URI dereferencing, a crawler for local decisions and 2 reuse examples on top of the harvested data.
